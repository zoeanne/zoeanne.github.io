Revamping the Map Collection: The University of Chicago - The John Crerar Library

I volunteered for this project through the Alternative Spring Break program. The main goal of this project was to revamp the map collection website to make it more usable for a 21st century audience. The original website was all hardcoded in HTML by the previous map collection specialist who had retired. Over the course of the week we collaborated with librarians at the John Crerar Library and Regenstein Library to become more familiar with the map collection content, LibGuide best practices, and User Experience methods used in the library.
Our process included migrating all of the content from the old website to a LibGuide, and aligning it with the visual identity of the University of Chicago Library. We conducted heuristics evaluations and an XO test to ensure usability of the site.
By the end of the week we had created a publishable LibGuide for the Map Collection website. We were able to make an impact at the library by creating this usable tool while learning more about information architecture, library science, and user experience.

UX Case Study: Hooked Deals App

For SI 422: Needs Assessment and Usability Evaluation, my semester long project was to evaluate the Hooked Deals App which is a money saving mobile app for food deals on campus. My team and I conducted competitive analysis, heuristic evaluations, A/B testing, usability tests and surveys, and personas and scenerios. Our goal was to understand the apps intended target audience and the environment surrounding the app and it's competitors so that we could redesign and improve the functionality of the Hooked.
